#ATeamComeTrue – Mercedes-AMG Petronas Motorsport write Formula One history with their sixth consecutive double title

- Mercedes-AMG Petronas Motorsport win the FIA Formula One Constructors’ Championship for the sixth time in a row

- Lewis and Valtteri are the only two drivers who can still win the Drivers’ Championship, securing the sixth consecutive Drivers’ Title for the team as well – making Mercedes-AMG Petronas Motorsport the first team in Formula One history to win both titles six times in a row

- Valtteri claimed his sixth victory in Formula One, his third of the 2019 season and first at the Japanese Grand Prix

- Lewis finished the race in P3 claiming an additional point for the fastest lap – his fourth fastest lap at the Japanese Grand Prix, equalling Michael Schumacher’s record for the most fastest laps in Japan

- Today’s result marks the 100th points finish for Valtteri

- Lewis (338 points) leads the Drivers’ Championship by 64 points from Valtteri (274 points) with Charles Leclerc (221) a further 53 points behind, giving only our two drivers the chance to win the 2019 Drivers’ Title

- Mercedes-AMG Petronas Motorsport (612 points) leads Ferrari (435 points) by 177 points – an unassailable lead in the Constructors’ Championship

2019 United States Grand Prix: Lewis Hamilton wins the 2019 FIA Formula One Drivers’ Championship, bringing his total number to 6, one less than Michael Schumacher.
